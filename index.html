I’m working on ARC-AGI-3 and building open-source RL environments. Most of my work is evaluation infrastructure, RL environments, and GPU-level performance tooling for AI systems—making execution paths trustworthy first and fast immediately after.

1Selected Work

1.1Zerobrew

I made Zerobrew [2] cold installs 3.6× faster (361 s → 99 s) with racing cancellation and HTTP/2 tuning. The change was merged to main and shipped to every user.

1.2Evaluation environments

I built six reproducible evaluation environments (safety, web agents, tool use/MCP, and a game suite) for Prime Intellect’s Community Environments [3], keeping rollout, verifier, and scoring paths separate so failures are easy to attribute.

1.3GPU kernels

I reached 12.804 µs on an NVIDIA B200 in GPU Mode’s nvfp4 block-scaled GEMM hackathon [4], writing CuTeDSL kernels aimed at speed-of-light performance.

1.4OpenGraphs

I built OpenGraphs [5] at Build India Hack (Anthropic × Replit × Lightspeed): a local-first, TUI-native experiment tracker for AI runs over SSH, with live graphs, logs, and run comparison on a Rust-first stack.

3Research
